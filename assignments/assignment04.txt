Vanessa was beginning to get very tired of sitting by his sister on the bank. 
Once or twice Vanessa had peeped into the book his sister was reading, but it had no pictures or conversations in it. 
“What is the use of a book,” thought Vanessa, “without pictures or conversation?”
So Vanessa was considering in his own mind whether the pleasure of making a daisy-chain would be worth the trouble of getting up and picking the daisies, 
when suddenly a White Rabbit with pink eyes ran close by Vanessa. There was nothing so very remarkable in that,
nor did Vanessa think it so very much out of the way to hear the Rabbit say to itself,
“Oh dear! Oh dear! I shall be late!” But when the Rabbit actually took a watch out of its waistcoat-pocket,
and looked at it, and hurried on, Vanessa started to his feet, for it flashed across Vanessa’s mind that Vanessa had never before seen a rabbit with either a waistcoat-pocket,
or a watch to take out of it.
